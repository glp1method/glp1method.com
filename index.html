The Missing Piece

The medication opens a window.
Most people don't know it's there.

GLP-1 medications are genuinely remarkable. They reduce food noise, lower cravings, and give your brain a break from the constant pull of highly rewarding foods.

But here's what nobody tells you: the medication doesn't change the emotional patterns, thought loops, or coping habits that drove your relationship with food in the first place.

As a licensed therapist, I've worked with people who lose significant weight on GLP-1s ‚Äî and still feel out of control around food emotionally. The biology changed. The psychology didn't.

This workbook is what bridges that gap. It uses the same clinical tools I use with therapy clients ‚Äî CBT, Motivational Interviewing, behavioral activation ‚Äî translated into exercises you can work through on your own.

Without the psychological work, most people experience:

  • Emotional eating that returns as soon as stress hits ‚Äî regardless of the medication
  • The same food noise patterns re-emerging when the dose changes
  • Significant anxiety about ever stopping the medication
  • Weight regain after stopping, because nothing underlying changed
  • Shame cycles: overeating, guilt, restriction, repeat
  • A relationship with food that still feels broken, even with progress on the scale
